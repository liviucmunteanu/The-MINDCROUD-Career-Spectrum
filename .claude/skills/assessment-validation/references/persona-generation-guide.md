# Persona Generation Guide

## Purpose

This guide defines how to generate 120 custom personas tailored to a specific assessment instrument. Personas are the core stress-testing mechanism — they simulate how real humans with diverse backgrounds, psychologies, and motivations would interact with the instrument.

## Persona Profile Format

Every persona must include all of the following fields:

```
**Persona [ID]: [Full Name]**
Age: [age] | [Gender] | [Cultural/ethnic background] | [City, Country] | [Education]
[Current role], [industry], [years of experience]
Big Five: E-[Level], C-[Level], A-[Level], N-[Level], O-[Level]
[Key motivational drivers and behavioral patterns — 1-2 sentences]
PURPOSE: [What this persona is designed to test or reveal about the instrument]
TENDENCY: [How this person would likely approach the assessment — response patterns]
```

Levels for Big Five: Very Low, Low, Moderate, High, Very High

## Tailoring Personas to the Instrument

Before generating personas, analyze the instrument to identify:

1. **Target population**: Who is this instrument designed for? (e.g., mid-career professionals, students, leaders, all workers)
2. **Domain**: What does it measure? (career fit, leadership style, personality, engagement, etc.)
3. **Constructs/dimensions**: What are the scales? Each needs personas that score at extremes and in between.
4. **Classification system**: If it assigns types/archetypes, generate "pure type" personas plus deliberate ties.
5. **Assumed context**: Does it assume corporate employment, specific industries, seniority levels?
6. **Cultural frame**: Does it assume Western/individualistic values?
7. **Response format**: Likert scale? Forced choice? This affects how personas respond.

## Category A: Career/Domain Stage Spectrum (Personas 1-20)

Generate 20 personas spanning the full range of experience levels relevant to the instrument's target population.

### Required coverage:
- **4-5 early-stage** (0-3 years experience): Include at least one first-generation professional, one career uncertain, one high-confidence early achiever
- **4-5 rising professionals** (4-10 years): Include hybrid roles, cross-functional, someone in a non-traditional path
- **4-5 established professionals** (10-20 years): Include different industries, leadership styles, and cultural contexts
- **3-4 senior/late-career** (20+ years): Include someone considering retirement, a career pivoter, and an executive
- **2-3 non-traditional trajectories**: Career gaps, late starters, career changers

### Testing purposes to cover:
- Can the instrument differentiate genuine low confidence from low competence? (Persona who understates abilities)
- Does the instrument work for someone whose role doesn't match its assumed context? (Non-corporate, blue-collar, freelance)
- Can early-career respondents with limited experience answer meaningfully? (Floor effects)
- Are achievement items biased toward mid-career professionals? (Career stage bias)

## Category B: Industry & Function Diversity (Personas 21-40)

Generate 20 personas spanning industries and job functions that test the instrument's breadth.

### Required coverage:
- At least 3 non-corporate professionals (trades, healthcare, education, agriculture, military, arts)
- At least 2 entrepreneurs or self-employed
- At least 2 public sector / government / NGO
- At least 2 from emerging markets or non-Western business cultures
- At least 2 from highly specialized technical roles
- At least 2 from people-centric roles (HR, coaching, social work, nursing)
- At least 1 academic/researcher
- At least 1 gig economy / portfolio career

### Testing purposes to cover:
- Does the instrument's language alienate non-corporate respondents?
- Are items meaningful for people outside knowledge work?
- Can specialists and generalists both be accurately profiled?
- Does the instrument handle people whose work context doesn't involve traditional teams or hierarchies?

## Category C: Psychological Profile Variations (Personas 41-60)

Generate 20 personas that test the instrument's psychological sensitivity.

### Required coverage:
- **Extreme Big Five profiles**: At least one persona at each extreme of each Big Five factor (10 personas minimum covering the 5 factors × 2 extremes)
- **Meta-aware respondent**: Someone who knows psychometrics and will give analytically filtered answers
- **High-performer with low agreeableness**: Tests whether effectiveness without warmth is captured
- **Entrepreneur with contradictory traits**: High initiative + low structure (common real pattern)
- **Someone who actively devalues some dimensions**: Tests how dismissive respondents are profiled
- **Someone effective BECAUSE they avoid a valued behavior**: e.g., effective because they avoid collaboration
- **A genuinely balanced person**: Tests whether "balanced" is a real profile or a default catch-all

### Testing purposes to cover:
- Can the instrument handle contradictory but authentic profiles?
- Does it distinguish between preference and capability?
- Does it penalize personality traits (introversion, low agreeableness) that aren't actually deficits?
- Can it identify people who are effective through unconventional means?

## Category D: Edge Cases & Stress Tests (Personas 61-80)

Generate 20 personas designed to break or expose weaknesses in the instrument.

### Required coverage (these are essential stress tests):
- **Impression Manager**: Someone strategically inflating all responses (+1-2 on everything). Tests social desirability detection.
- **Disengaged Completer**: All responses around the midpoint. Tests whether the instrument catches apathy vs. genuine balance.
- **Acquiescence Bias**: Someone who agrees with all positively worded statements regardless of content. Tests whether lack of reverse-scored items is a problem.
- **Extreme Responder**: Only uses endpoints of the scale (1s and 7s, never middle). Tests extreme response bias detection.
- **Career Changer**: Current role and natural tendencies point in different directions. Tests temporal frame confusion.
- **Military/Institutional Transitioner**: Experience from a structured institution applied to a different context.
- **Returning Professional**: After a significant career break. Tests whether the instrument creates false "development needs."
- **Gig Worker / No Single Role**: No fixed employer, multiple simultaneous roles. Tests organizational context dependency.
- **Autistic Professional**: Highly effective but low on social/networking items. Tests whether neurodivergent traits are pathologized.
- **ADHD Professional**: Very high initiative, very low structure. Tests contradictory patterns.
- **Currently Depressed High-Performer**: Temporary state suppressing true trait levels. Tests state vs. trait sensitivity.
- **Over-Qualified for Current Role**: Capabilities far exceed current position. Tests role-person fit items.
- **Code-Switcher**: Operates across two cultural frames. Tests cultural fluidity items.
- **Burned-Out Idealist**: High values but depleted engagement. Tests values vs. motivation separation.
- **Person Who Scores High on Opposing Traits**: e.g., both innovative AND process-oriented. Tests false dichotomies.
- **Silent Leader**: Effective leader who is extremely introverted. Tests whether leadership = extraversion.
- **Post-Career Professional**: Retired, volunteering/mentoring. Tests age ceiling.
- **Remote-Native Worker**: Has never worked in an office. Tests in-person assumptions.
- **Political Operator**: Career built on influence, not delivery. Tests whether political intelligence is captured.
- **Impostor Syndrome Case**: Objectively excellent, systematically underrates self. Tests self-perception distortion.

## Category E: Demographic Diversity & Representation (Personas 81-100)

Generate 20 personas that test fairness and bias across demographic dimensions.

### Required coverage:
- At least 2 personas testing gender bias in items
- At least 3 testing cultural/ethnic background bias (include non-Western cultures)
- At least 2 testing age bias (one younger, one older)
- At least 2 testing disability/chronic illness impact (physical and neurological)
- At least 2 testing socioeconomic/class assumptions
- At least 1 testing religious/values framework differences
- At least 1 testing non-traditional education pathways
- At least 1 testing caregiving constraints on career items
- At least 1 testing immigration/credential displacement
- At least 1 testing formerly marginalized backgrounds
- At least 1 testing non-binary or trans professional experience
- At least 1 testing Indigenous or non-Western career frameworks

### Testing purposes to cover:
- Do items assume unlimited freedom to pursue development?
- Do communication items assume hearing/speaking ability?
- Do leadership items penalize non-Western leadership styles?
- Do career growth items create unfair pressure for constrained individuals?
- Do items assume a specific class or educational background?

## Category F: Classification Stress Tests (Personas 101-120)

Generate 20 personas specifically designed to test the classification/typology algorithm.

### Required coverage:
- **One "pure" persona per classification category**: If the instrument has 8 types, generate 8 personas engineered to score maximally on each type's defining dimensions. These test whether the algorithm correctly assigns the intended type when the profile is unambiguous.
- **Deliberate ties**: For each pair of types that share dimensions, generate one persona that should produce near-identical scores for both. These test tie-breaking logic.
- **Flat profile**: Every item answered at the midpoint (e.g., 4 on a 7-point scale). Tests what happens with no differentiation.
- **Inverted profile**: Every dimension either very high or very low, no middle ground. Tests polarized profiles.
- **Random responder**: Pseudo-random pattern (alternate 2,4,5,6,3,7,1). Tests whether quality assurance catches invalid responses.
- **True balanced integrator**: Genuinely moderate on everything (not apathetic, but authentically balanced). Tests whether "balanced" is a real type or a default.

If the instrument has N classification categories, generate:
- N pure-type personas (one per category)
- At least 3 tie-breaker personas (targeting the most likely confusion pairs)
- 1 flat profile
- 1 inverted profile
- 1 random responder
- 1 true balanced integrator
- Fill remaining slots with additional tie-breakers or edge cases

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing the persona bank, verify:

- [ ] All 120 personas have complete profiles (demographics, Big Five, purpose, tendency)
- [ ] Every dimension in the instrument is stressed at both extremes by at least 2 personas
- [ ] Every classification category has at least 1 pure-type persona
- [ ] At least 15 personas are from non-Western cultural contexts
- [ ] At least 10 personas represent marginalized or underrepresented groups
- [ ] At least 5 personas test response biases (impression management, acquiescence, extreme responding)
- [ ] At least 5 personas have neurodivergent profiles
- [ ] At least 5 personas have non-traditional career paths
- [ ] Each category of 20 personas has diverse gender representation
- [ ] Each category includes at least 3 different countries of residence
- [ ] No two personas have identical Big Five profiles
- [ ] Every persona has a unique, specific testing purpose
