# Deliverable Templates

This reference contains the full templates for all supporting deliverables the skill
can generate. Each section provides the required structure and content guidance.

## 1. Administration Manual

Generate a complete manual with these 12 sections:

### Section 1: Assessment Overview
- Assessment name, version, date, author
- Purpose and intended uses
- Theoretical framework and construct definition
- Target population
- Appropriate and inappropriate uses (explicit list of both)

### Section 2: Qualifications for Administration
- Required level of training (Level A: general / Level B: training in psychometrics /
  Level C: licensed psychologist)
- Recommended for this assessment: specify based on stakes level
- Training resources or prerequisites

### Section 3: Materials Required
- Assessment booklet/online platform specifications
- Scoring materials (manual scoring key or software)
- Timing equipment (if timed)
- Supplementary materials (pencils, calculators, etc.)

### Section 4: Pre-Administration Procedures
- Scheduling guidance
- Environmental requirements (quiet room, adequate lighting, no distractions)
- Informed consent process (include consent form template)
- Accommodations for special needs (extended time, large print, screen reader
  compatibility, separate room)
- Rapport-building script

### Section 5: Standardized Instructions (Verbatim Scripts)
Provide word-for-word scripts for:
- Introduction and purpose explanation
- Confidentiality statement
- Instructions for completing the assessment
- Practice items walkthrough
- "Any questions?" checkpoint
- Begin assessment signal
- Time warnings (if timed): "You have 10 minutes remaining"
- Completion instructions

Example opening script:
```
"Thank you for participating in this assessment. The purpose of [Assessment Name]
is to help us understand [purpose in plain language]. There are no right or wrong
answers — we are interested in your honest responses. Your individual results will
be [confidentiality terms]. The assessment contains [N] questions and should take
approximately [time] to complete. Please read each statement carefully and select
the response that best describes you. Do you have any questions before we begin?"
```

### Section 6: Administration Procedures
- Individual vs group administration protocols
- Online administration: platform requirements, browser compatibility, internet requirements
- Paper administration: booklet handling, answer sheet instructions
- Timing protocols (start/stop procedures, what to do if interrupted)
- Handling questions during administration (standard response: "Please answer based
  on your first impression")
- What to do if respondent becomes distressed

### Section 7: Scoring Procedures
- Step-by-step scoring instructions
- Reverse-scoring item list and procedure
- Subscale computation formulas
- Total/composite score computation
- Score transformation tables or formulas
- Handling missing data
- Validity indicator computation and flagging thresholds

### Section 8: Score Interpretation
- Norm tables (or reference to norm supplement)
- Score classification bands with descriptive labels
- Profile interpretation guidelines
- Confidence intervals and how to explain them
- Common profile patterns and their meanings
- Cautions about over-interpretation

### Section 9: Psychometric Properties
- Reliability: internal consistency (α/ω by subscale), test-retest (if available),
  inter-rater (if applicable)
- Validity: content (CVI, expert panel), construct (factor analysis results),
  criterion (correlations with external criteria)
- Standard Error of Measurement by subscale
- Item analysis summary statistics

### Section 10: Fairness and Bias
- DIF analysis results by demographic group
- Measurement invariance results
- Accommodations and their impact on scores
- Cultural considerations for interpretation
- Statement on non-discriminatory use

### Section 11: Ethical Guidelines
- Informed consent requirements
- Confidentiality and data storage
- Right to feedback
- Qualified user requirements
- Prohibition on use for unintended purposes
- Data retention and destruction policies
- GDPR/local data protection compliance

### Section 12: References and Appendices
- Theoretical references
- Technical development references
- Norm table appendix
- Consent form template
- Score report template
- Frequently asked questions

## 3. Individual Report Template (8 Sections)

### Section 1: Administrative Information
- Respondent name/ID (or anonymous identifier)
- Assessment date, version
- Administration context (online/paper, proctored/unproctored)
- Norm group used for comparison
- Report generation date, qualified interpreter

### Section 2: Executive Summary
- 3–4 sentence overview of key findings
- Highlight top 2–3 strengths and 1–2 development areas
- Overall profile characterization
- Tone: constructive, strengths-first, balanced

### Section 3: Validity Indicators
- Completion rate
- Response consistency index
- Social desirability score (if applicable)
- Infrequency indicators
- Statement: "These indicators suggest the results [are/may not be] a valid
  representation of the respondent's actual [construct]."

### Section 4: Score Profile
- Visual display: bar chart or radar plot showing all dimension scores
- Include confidence interval bands
- Normative comparison group clearly labeled
- Color coding: use accessible colors (not red/green only — include patterns for
  colorblind accessibility)

### Section 5: Detailed Dimension Analysis
For each dimension (in order of relevance or strength):
- Score and classification (e.g., "Sten 8 — High")
- Percentile rank ("Higher than 89% of the comparison group")
- Narrative interpretation (2–3 sentences explaining what this means behaviorally)
- Facet-level breakdown (if applicable)

### Section 6: Strengths and Development Areas
- **Top Strengths** (highest 2–3 scores): How to leverage these
- **Development Opportunities** (lowest 2–3 scores): Specific, actionable suggestions
- Frame all feedback constructively — "development opportunity" not "weakness"

### Section 7: Actionable Recommendations
- 3–5 specific development actions tied to profile results
- Each recommendation includes: what to do, why it matters (linked to score),
  and how to start
- SMART goal framework where appropriate

### Section 8: Important Notes
- Confidentiality statement
- Assessment limitations
- Recommendation for follow-up discussion with qualified professional
- Data retention policy
- Contact information for questions

## 4. Group Report Template (6 Sections)

### Section 1: Group Overview
- Group description (team, department, cohort, etc.)
- Number of respondents
- Response rate and completion quality
- Norm group used
- Date range of administration

### Section 2: Aggregate Score Summary
- Mean, SD, and range for each dimension
- Visual: grouped bar chart or box plot showing distribution per dimension
- Comparison to norm group (if applicable)
- Overall group profile characterization

### Section 3: Distribution Analysis
- Score distribution for each dimension (histogram or box plot)
- Identify dimensions with high variance (potential subgroup differences)
- Identify dimensions with high consensus (shared group characteristic)

### Section 4: Subgroup Comparisons
- Compare scores across relevant subgroups (role level, department, tenure, etc.)
- Minimum n ≥ 5 per subgroup for anonymity
- Report effect sizes (Cohen's d) for meaningful differences (d ≥ 0.50)
- Use caution language for small subgroups

### Section 5: Pattern Analysis
- Identify common profile patterns within the group
- Cluster analysis summary (if applicable): how many distinct profile types exist
- Implications of profile diversity for team composition and dynamics

### Section 6: Strategic Recommendations
- Group-level strengths to leverage
- Group-level development priorities
- Team composition insights (complementary profiles, gaps, redundancies)
- Recommended interventions (training, coaching, structural changes)
- Benchmarking against industry norms (if available)

## 5. Debrief/Feedback Session Guide

### Session Structure (60–90 minutes)

#### Phase 1: Preparation (Before Session — 15 min)
- Review individual report thoroughly
- Identify 2–3 key strengths and 1–2 development areas
- Prepare follow-up questions for surprising or extreme scores
- Prepare 2–3 initial development suggestions
- Check validity indicators — if any are flagged, prepare how to address

#### Phase 2: Opening (10 min)
- **Build rapport**: Warm greeting, small talk, comfortable environment
- **Set the frame**: "This session is developmental, not evaluative. The assessment
  gives us a starting point for a conversation about your strengths and growth areas."
- **Clarify confidentiality**: Who will see the results, how they'll be used
- **Set expectations**: Session length, structure, their role (active participant, not
  passive recipient)

#### Phase 3: Initial Reactions (10 min)
- Share the score profile visual (bar chart or radar plot)
- Ask: "What stands out to you?" / "Does anything surprise you?"
- Let them react naturally — their initial response reveals self-awareness
- Note agreements and disagreements with the data

#### Phase 4: Strengths Deep-Dive (15 min)
- **Always start with strengths** — this sets a constructive tone
- Walk through top 2–3 scores
- For each: explain what the score means → ask for behavioral examples →
  explore how they leverage this strength → discuss where it might become
  a liability in excess
- Validate their strengths with specific examples from their experience

#### Phase 5: Development Areas (15 min)
- Transition naturally: "Now let's look at some areas where there's room for growth..."
- Walk through 1–2 lower scores
- For each: explain what the score means → normalize it ("many [role type] score
  similarly here") → ask if they've noticed this → explore barriers and enablers
- Frame as opportunities, not deficits

#### Phase 6: Integration and Goal Setting (15 min)
- Help synthesize the profile as a whole: "When I look at your overall pattern,
  what I see is someone who [integrated narrative]..."
- Collaborate on 1–3 development goals (SMART format):
  - Specific: What exactly will you do?
  - Measurable: How will you know you've improved?
  - Achievable: Is this realistic given your context?
  - Relevant: Does this matter for your career/team/organization?
  - Time-bound: By when?
- Identify specific actions for the next 30/60/90 days

#### Phase 7: Closing (5 min)
- Summarize key takeaways
- Confirm development goals
- Agree on follow-up (when, how, with whom)
- Invite final questions
- Thank them for their openness

### Cardinal Rules for Debrief
1. **Always start with strengths.** Never lead with deficits.
2. **Use the data as a conversation starter, not a verdict.** The assessment is one
   data point alongside their experience, context, and self-knowledge.
3. **Ask more than you tell.** The best debriefs are 60% listening, 40% sharing.
4. **Normalize.** "Many people in your role score similarly on this dimension" reduces
   defensiveness.
5. **Connect to their goals.** Development areas only matter if they're relevant to
   what the person wants to achieve.
6. **Never diagnose.** Assessment results are behavioral patterns, not clinical diagnoses.
7. **Honor disagreement.** If they don't recognize themselves in a score, explore why
   rather than insisting the data is right.

## 6. Technical Appendix

When detailed psychometric documentation is requested, include:

```markdown
# Technical Appendix: [Assessment Name]

## Development History
- Version history and revision dates
- Item pool development process
- Expert panel composition and CVI results
- Pilot study details (sample, findings, revisions)

## Item Analysis
| Item ID | Dimension | Keying | M | SD | Corrected r-it | α if deleted | p-value |
|---------|-----------|--------|---|----|----|----|----|
[Full item analysis table]

## Reliability Evidence
| Subscale | Items | α | ω | SEM | 95% CI Width |
|----------|-------|---|---|-----|--------------|
[Reliability by subscale]

Test-Retest: [interval, N, ICC by subscale]
Inter-Rater (if applicable): [ICC, % agreement]

## Validity Evidence
### Content Validity
- Expert panel (N, qualifications)
- I-CVI per item, S-CVI/Ave for scale

### Construct Validity
- EFA: method, rotation, eigenvalues, scree, factor loadings
- CFA: model, fit indices (χ², df, CFI, TLI, RMSEA, SRMR), factor loadings

### Criterion Validity
- Convergent: correlations with related measures
- Discriminant: correlations with unrelated measures
- Predictive: correlations with future criteria (if available)

## Norm Tables
[Full percentile, T-score, or sten tables by demographic group]

## Fairness Evidence
- DIF analysis: method, groups compared, results
- Measurement invariance: levels tested, fit statistics, conclusions
- Accommodation studies (if applicable)

## Validation Recommendations
[What additional studies are needed for full empirical validation]
```
